Track 01

Research

My research investigates the application of semantic web technologies, ontological modelling, and knowledge graphs to public administration performance management. The work originates in practice — the MSc thesis documents a full implementation at Portugal's Secretaria-Geral do Governo — and continues through a PhD programme at ISCTE-IUL (2025–2029).

Outputs are documented chronologically. Each repository presents the research question, methodology, findings, and academic context. Full implementation code and sensitive institutional data are maintained in private repositories available on request.

Completed Outputs

MSc Thesis · ISCTE-IUL · November 2025

Balanced Scorecard Ontology Implementation at Secretaria-Geral do Governo

This thesis documents the design, implementation, and evaluation of a Balanced Scorecard Ontology (BSO) at Portugal's Secretaria-Geral do Governo. Leveraging semantic web technologies — including OWL, RDF, SPARQL, and GraphDB — alongside large language models for ontological transformation, the system achieved a 96% strategic objective completion rate and a 72% reduction in strategic analysis time. Context

Research Focus

The central question driving this research is how semantic technologies and knowledge graph architectures can improve strategic performance management in complex, multi-entity public sector environments — where data interoperability, reasoning transparency, and institutional accountability are persistent challenges.

The BSO implementation at SG-Gov demonstrated that ontological modelling, combined with automated reasoning and LLM-assisted transformation pipelines, can produce measurable improvements in both completion rates and analytical efficiency at scale. The PhD programme builds on this foundation, developing a more generalisable theoretical and practical framework.

Access to private repositories. Full implementation source code, internal datasets, and SG-Gov strategic indicators are maintained privately. Academic or professional collaborators wishing to access this material are welcome to connect via LinkedIn and request access with a brief description of their interest.
